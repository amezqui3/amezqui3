About

I am a Computational Mathematics, Science and Engineering (CMSE) PhD student at Michigan State University. I'm interested in understanding plant morphology using topological data analysis (TDA). My advisors are Dr. Dan Chitwood and Dr. Elizabeth Munch. I did my math degree at Universidad de Guanajuato with extensive support from the Mathematics Research Center (CIMAT). Previously, I tried to quantify the morphology of pre-Columbian masks with TDA, and how this might reveal their culture of origin. This archaeological project constituted my bachelor thesis under the guidance of Dr. Antonio Rieser.

Research interests: Topological Data Analysis (TDA), Euler Characteristic Transform, X-ray CT scans, image processing, plant morphology.
